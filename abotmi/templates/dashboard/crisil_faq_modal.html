D&B Verification (DBV)

1. Who can apply to become a D&B Verified Advisor (DBV)?

Only an advisor who has submitted all necessary documents and successfully completed registration is eligible to apply to be a DBV. An advisor needs to be a Registered ABOTMI to apply.

2. Who is a D&B Verified Advisor (DBV)?

Every registered advisor who receives a D&B verification certificate upon successful verification is awarded the title of DBV.

3. What is D&B verification?

It is a process to verify and certify Real estate Investment Advisors. ABOTMI has an exclusive tie-up with D&B - India’s leading rating agency. All the advisors who register with ABOTMI have this unique opportunity to get them verified by D&B.

Based on the self declaration made by registered advisors, D&B will verify the credibility of the advisors with various statutory and regulatory authorities. Advisors or an Advisory firm can choose to apply this Verification.

A certificate will be published upon successful D&B verification.

4. How to apply for D&B verification?

Advisor can begin the process of application only after registration. He can click on the ‘Apply Now’ button under the DBV application tab on Advisor dashboard (My Page).

5. Why should you apply for D&B verification?

There are 5 significant reasons why an advisor should apply for D&B verification.

6. Is D&B verification done free of cost?

No, a registered advisor will have to make payment to ABOTMI to apply for D&B verification.

7. What is the fee an Advisor will have to pay to apply for D&B verification?

An advisor will have to make a payment of Rs.11000/- plus applicable taxes to apply for D&B verification.

8. What will the advisor receive upon successful completion of D&B verification?

Advisor will receive a certificate recognizing him as a D&B Verified Advisor’ (DBV). The same will also be updated on his Digital profile (My Track). The advisor can download the certificate and use it to increase credibility in the advisory space.

9. What is the validity of the DBV certificate?

DBV certification is valid for the tenure for which the application was made by the advisor. Currently, an advisor can apply for 1, 2 or 3 years at a time.

10. How to renew DBV Certification?

DBV certification will have to be renewed upon expiry of the subscribed tenure. The renewal process in similar to a new application.

11. What are the special circumstances to renew D&B Certification?

Under the following situations, D&B verification will cease to be valid even before the completion of the subscribed tenure and will have to be renewed upon additional payment and inspection:

    1.If the advisor changes his business location from one place to another during the year.

    2.If the advisor changes the organization that he works for during the year.

    3.If the advisor makes any change in the KYC documents provided to ABOTMI/D&B during the year.

    4.If the advisor makes any change in his communication or business address, e-mail and mobile number during the year.

    5.If the advisor adds any certification details such as SEBI, NISM, IRDA etc. during the year.

The advisor will have to make a payment of Rs.6750/- plus taxes whenever an event as mentioned above occurs. This is to ensure that D&B verification is up to date and valid with current information at any given point of the time in the year.

12. Are there any discounts or offers for Advisors applying for DBV?

Currently, Advisors are being offered ‘Pay 2 Get 3’ offer. Under this offer, advisors applying for DBV recognition for a term of 2 years will be offered an extra year of DBV certification free of cost. Therefore, on payment of 2 years DBV fee, an advisor gets 3 years of DBV certification.

Advisor Check

Advisor Check is a free tool from ABOTMI to help you research the professional backgrounds of brokers and brokerage firms, as well as investment advisory firms and advisors registered in www.abotmi.com.

Where does the information come from?

The information about brokers and brokerage firms that you find in advisor check comes from the Central Registration depository and also the self declaration made by them. All brokers must be licensed and registered by various regulatory authorities in India as well as globally wherever they are practicing financial advisory. Information is obtained through the forms that brokers, brokerage firms and regulators complete as part of their registration and licensing process. D&B obtains this after the advisors confirm to be verified and certified.

The information about investment advisory firms and representatives comes from the specific registration databases disclosed by the advisor after they give their confirmation to verify.

What will you find in a Verified Advisor report?

An ‘advisor verified’ report for a broker/ investment advisor who is currently registered with any regulator for financial investment advisory, or who has been registered within the last 10 years, contains:

  • A Report Summary that provides a brief overview of the broker / advisor and his or her credentials.
  • A Broker Qualifications section that includes a listing of the broker / advisor’s current registrations or licenses, if any, and industry exams he or she has passed.
  • A Registration and Employment History section that consists of:
    1. A list of registered securities, investment advisory firms where the broker is currently and/or was previously registered.
    2. The broker's employment history for the last 10 years—both within and outside the securities industry—as reported by the broker securities firms used to register brokers based on self-regulatory disclosure and full-time education.
  • A Disclosure section that includes information about customer disputes, disciplinary events and financial matters on the broker's record. Some of these items may involve pending actions or allegations that have not been resolved or proven, only if those are very serious.
  • The broker's most recently submitted comment(s), if any.
  • A Disclosure section that includes information about certain criminal, regulatory, civil judicial or customer complaint events involving the broker. For an event to be included in an advisory verified report, the broker must have been:
    1. the subject of a final regulatory action;
    2. convicted of or pled guilty or no contest to certain crimes;
    3. subject to a civil injunction involving investment-related activity or found in a civil court to have been involved in a violation of investment-related statutes or regulations; or
    4. named as a respondent or defendant in an arbitration or civil litigation in which the broker was alleged to have committed a sales practice violation, and which resulted in an award or civil judgment against the broker.
    5. The broker’s most recently submitted comment(s), if any.

A Verification Report for an advisory firm contains:

  • A Report Summary that provides a brief overview of the firm and its background.
  • A Firm Profile that describes where and when the firm was established, and lists the people and organizations that own controlling shares or directly influence the firm's daily operations.
  • A Firm History that details any mergers, acquisitions or name changes affecting the firm.
  • A Firm Operations section that lists the firm's active licenses and registrations, the types of businesses it conducts and other details pertaining to its operations.
  • A Disclosure section that contains information about any arbitration awards, disciplinary events, and financial matters on the firm's record. Some of these items may involve pending actions or allegations that have not been resolved or proven.

Generally, active firms and brokers are required to update their professional and disciplinary information within 30 days.

What information is NOT disclosed through Advisor Verification?

Information that are personal or confidential (for example, residential addresses), and those that are no longer required to be reported (for example, judgments or liens that have been satisfied) are not disclosed through the Verification Report.

In addition, D&B reserves the right to exclude information that contains confidential customer information, offensive and potentially defamatory language or information that raises significant identity theft or privacy concerns that are not outweighed by investor protection concerns.

ABOTMI’s public disclosure policy, in certain limited circumstances (most often pursuant to a court order) information may be expunged from the system.
